### 🌙 About Me
I am **Huajiang Wang**, a computer science master's student. Instead of just calling myself a "programmer," I prefer to think of myself as someone who tries to make the walls of the information world a bit lower.

I often feel that the tech world likes to "raise its voice" — using layers of jargon and acronyms to prove how advanced it is. What really moves me, though, are people who can explain hard things in plain words. To me, **speaking clearly takes more skill than speaking in a mysterious way**. So my work is simple: remove the extra wrapping and let the structure of technology be seen.

- 🎓 **Identity**: Master's student (Class of 2027), staying calm in the AI fever.
- 🎯 **What I care about**: Not using tech as decoration, but solving real problems.
- 🌐 **Personal site**: [huajiang.wang](http://huajiang.wang)

### 🌿 Why Write This Blog?
There is already more than enough information in the world, so one more blog is not a "must." But if it can be a small, quiet place in the noise, then it may be worth keeping.

You will not find copy-paste tutorials here. What I hope to leave are thoughts that have been slowly worked through:

- **Disenchantment of AI**: Bringing concepts like Agent and MCP down from the altar and putting them back on the table, to see what they can and cannot do in real cases.
- **The Essence of Engineering**: Code should run, of course. But more important is how it keeps a long-term, ordered relationship with the world. From Vue to React, tools change, but the care for structure, boundaries, and maintainability does not.
